## What is CEH?

**Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)** is a professional certification provided by the EC-Council (International Council of E-Commerce Consultants) that demonstrates an individual's ability to assess the security of computer systems by looking for weaknesses and vulnerabilities in target systems, using the same tools and knowledge as a malicious hacker. However, the key difference is that ethical hackers operate with permission and within legal bounds to identify and help fix security flaws before they can be exploited by malicious actors.

### Where is CEH used?
CEH is primarily used in the field of **cybersecurity**. Certified Ethical Hackers work in various industries such as:
- Government agencies
- Private corporations
- Financial institutions
- IT service providers
- Cybersecurity firms
- Security consultancies

They perform **penetration testing**, **vulnerability assessments**, and **network security audits** to ensure that an organization's information systems are secure from cyberattacks.

### Summary of CEH:
CEH certification equips professionals with the skills to legally and ethically hack into systems to identify vulnerabilities and help organizations safeguard their infrastructure. It is recognized globally as a key certification for IT and cybersecurity professionals, making it valuable for anyone pursuing a career in information security or those responsible for protecting sensitive data from cyber threats.
