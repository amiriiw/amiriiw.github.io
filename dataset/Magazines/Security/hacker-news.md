# What Hackers Read and Where They Share Information
Hackers typically use a wide variety of resources to gather information, stay updated, and share their findings. These resources can include underground forums, specialized websites, private chat channels, and specific tools for monitoring vulnerabilities. Here's a breakdown of where hackers get and share information:

## 1. **Underground Forums**
Hackers use underground forums to discuss hacking techniques, tools, and new vulnerabilities. Some well-known forums include:

- **[Exploit.in](https://exploit.in/)**: A popular underground forum for hackers.
- **[RaidForums](https://raidforums.com/)**: A forum for sharing hacked data and hacking techniques (currently inactive).
- **[BreachForums](https://breachforums.ru/)**: A place to buy and sell leaked or stolen data.

These forums are often difficult to access and may require invitations.

## 2. **Private Chat Channels**
Hackers communicate in private chat channels using platforms like **Telegram**, **Discord**, or **IRC (Internet Relay Chat)** to share sensitive information and hacking tactics. Many of these channels are invite-only and operate secretly.

- **[Telegram](https://telegram.org/)**: Used widely for private hacker groups.
- **[Discord](https://discord.com/)**: Popular among technical communities, including hacking groups.
- **[IRC Networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_Relay_Chat)**: Classic platform for real-time text communication in hacker communities.

## 3. **Hacking and Cybersecurity Websites**
Several websites provide up-to-date news on cybersecurity, vulnerabilities, and hacking techniques. These websites are useful both for hackers and security researchers:

- **[Exploit-DB](https://www.exploit-db.com/)**: A database of publicly known exploits.
- **[HackerOne](https://www.hackerone.com/)**: A platform where ethical hackers report bugs to companies.
- **[Bugcrowd](https://www.bugcrowd.com/)**: Similar to HackerOne, it’s a bug bounty platform.
- **[The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/)**: A trusted news source in the cybersecurity and hacking community.
- **[Hckr News](https://hckrnews.com/)**: A trusted news source in the cybersecurity and hacking community sorted from The Hacker News. 

## 4. **Social Media & Blogs**
Many hackers and cybersecurity researchers are active on social media platforms such as **Twitter (now X)**, **Reddit**, and **LinkedIn**, where they share the latest information on techniques, attacks, and vulnerabilities. Personal blogs are also common for publishing in-depth research.

- **[Twitter](https://twitter.com/)**: Follow prominent security experts and hacker accounts.
- **[Reddit - r/netsec](https://www.reddit.com/r/netsec/)**: A community for discussing security news and techniques.

## 5. **Educational Resources**
Hackers often use online courses and resources to learn new techniques and master various tools. Some of these educational resources include:

- **[Hack The Box](https://www.hackthebox.com/)**: A platform with virtual machines for hacking practice.
- **[TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com/)**: Another platform for learning cybersecurity skills.
- **[Offensive Security (OSCP)](https://www.offensive-security.com/)**: Provider of challenging hacking certifications, such as OSCP.

## 6. **Hacked Data Sources**
Hackers obtain sensitive information using various techniques such as:

- **Phishing**: Sending fake emails or messages to steal personal information.
- **Data Breaches**: Attacking organizations’ databases to steal data.
- **Ransomware Attacks**: Encrypting data and demanding a ransom.
- **Dark Web Marketplaces**: Selling stolen data on dark web markets like **[White House Market](https://dark.fail)** (now closed) or other marketplaces.

## 7. **Hacking & Monitoring Tools**
Hackers use various tools for monitoring networks and exploiting vulnerabilities. Some of the most common tools include:

- **[Nmap](https://nmap.org/)**: For scanning networks and finding open ports.
- **[Metasploit](https://www.metasploit.com/)**: A platform for running exploits and penetration testing.
- **[Wireshark](https://www.wireshark.org/)**: A network traffic analysis tool.
- **[John the Ripper](https://www.openwall.com/john/)**: Used for password cracking.

## 8. **Vulnerability Monitoring Tools**
There are specific tools and services that help hackers quickly discover new vulnerabilities:

- **[Shodan](https://www.shodan.io/)**: A search engine for finding internet-connected devices.
- **[CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures)](https://cve.mitre.org/)**: A database that lists known vulnerabilities.

## 9. **Reconnaissance (Information Gathering)**
Before launching an attack, hackers gather as much information as possible through:

- **[OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_intelligence)**: Using publicly available sources such as websites, social media, and public databases.
- **Social Engineering**: Tricking individuals into revealing sensitive information.

### Conclusion
Hackers use underground forums, private chat channels, specialized websites, social media, and various tools to stay updated and share knowledge. These resources help them to keep up with new techniques and exploit vulnerabilities effectively.
